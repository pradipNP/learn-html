Sanjay Dutt

Sanjay Balraj Dutt (born 29 July 1959)[4] is an Indian actor, playback singer and film producer who works in Hindi cinema in addition to a few Telugu, Kannada, Tamil , and Punjabi films. [5][6] He acted in over 135 films.
Dutt is regarded as one of the most popular actors of Indian cinema.[7] Known for his versatile roles, style and intensity, he has often portrayed flawed heroes on screen.
[8][9] Dutt won several accolades including two Filmfare Awards, three Screen Awards and a Global Indian Film Award.[10]

Part of the Dutt family, he is the son of actor-politicians Sunil Dutt and Nargis. Dutt made his acting debut with Rocky (1981), directed by his father. [11] A career slump followed, with major exceptions being Vidhaata (1982), Naam (1986) and Thanedaar (1990).[12] He later established himself as a Major Superstar with films like Saajan (1991) and Sadak (1991) and continued to deliver hits, such as Adharm (1992), Gumrah (1993), Khalnayak (1993), Daag: The Fire (1999), Haseena Maan Jaayegi (1999), Vaastav: The Reality (1999), Jodi No. 1 (2001), Munna Bhai MBBS (2003) and Lage Raho Munna Bhai (2006), the latter of which proved to be his biggest solo commercial success till date.[13][14] His critically acclaimed performance in Vaastav won him the Filmfare Award for Best Actor.[15] One of the most successful actors of the 1990s and 2000s, Dutt appeared in Box Office India's "Top Actors" list three times from 1991 to 1993. He topped the list twice in 1992 and 1993.[13]
